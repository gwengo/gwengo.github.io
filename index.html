Future Legends

NYU Clive Davis Gallery · February 2026

A month-long public exhibition asking who gets to imagine and archive technological futures. Originated and co-curated at NYU during Black History Month 2026, featuring scholars Ruha Benjamin, Ari Melenciano, and Salome Asega. The exhibition began as a 12-page proposal written before there was a gallery, a budget, or a confirmed artist, and was funded through institutional support from NYU departments, SGA, and CMEP.

Presented by Black Interactive in partnership with NYU Tisch, ITP, and CMEP. As AI and machine learning increasingly shape our collective future, who gets to imagine, design, and archive these futures becomes a critical question. Future Legends challenges predetermined narratives and asserts the right to imagine and create alternatives.

Exhibition documentation, press, and community archive.

WHERE DO WE GO

NYC Tech Week · June 2026 · Forthcoming

A research project and public event organized around a single question: at what moments do creative practitioners decide to use AI, refuse it, or work around it, and what's actually driving those decisions beneath the stated ideology?

The method is practitioner interviews conducted over the summer, recruited from New York's creative technology community and aimed for publication by end of summer.

Sponsored by a16z as part of NYC Tech Week 2026.

SubWave

DUMBO Projections · In progress

NYC is loud. A projection on the Manhattan Bridge in DUMBO turns live and historical neighborhood sounds into wave patterns that reveal relief prints of local historical and future wildlife. The East River (technically a tidal strait, not a river) does not flow in one direction, and this bidirectionality becomes the project's central metaphor: history is not linear, and ecological time does not move forward in a straight line.

Waves synchronized with the passing subway rise and recede, briefly surfacing images of harbor seals, humpback whales, striped bass, and oyster beds - species that have returned or could. The piece draws on the Billion Oyster Project, living shoreline research, and the ecological record of the East River to ask what recovery looks like when it is possible but not guaranteed.

With Ryan Webber and Rafia Santana.

The Office

ITP · 2025

A real-time, interactive 3D environment built in Unity staging the open-plan office as a terrarium. The mug, desk, monitor, and swivel chair form a catalog of corporate life and a vitrine of the knowledge worker's ecology.

Foliage invades the scene to surface nature as unruly presence. At the center is an overexposed figure as a form of burnout rendered as illumination.
